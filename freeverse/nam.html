Nam

The place of my uncle's birth,
and where he was stung
thirty years later by the war
which took the form of
a jellyfish and followed him across

Waters unanimous, An ocean of rising and falling hands,
Their palms cupped to catch moonlight
Their fists knocking in the dark.

From above, we commemorate
the underwater stargazers
with folksongs stitched into
the hemlines of our skirts,
longing to see the sun.

And the time when Ma bid us
goodbye forever,
neither of us knowing
forever.

And when we reached the other shore,
cutting away the threads
with the first pair
of scissors we could find.

How could we have said
not enough
opening the slips
of green paper to the sun?

To think of Ma by lamplight
the needle leading
the thread like a blind
pilgrim by the hand,
as the rest of us slept
in the cloth of night.

For we would wake
with the birds next morning,
and next morning
no sound greeted us
but sea splitting sea.

Something is lost among the street.
In a noodle shop it waits as a glass of
tap water westerners won't drink.

A generation passes,
this country still smells of smoke.
A generation passes
this country.
